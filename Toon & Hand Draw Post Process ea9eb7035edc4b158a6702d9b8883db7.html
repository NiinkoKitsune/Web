How to implement Post Process :

Firstly, add a Post Process Volume :

A 3D wireframed cube appears on the scene, it's the Post Process Volume.

  1. Click on the Wireframed cube in the scene or search for it in the Outliner.
  1. In the details, click on the PostProcessVolume.
  1. In the search bar, type "Material".
  1. In the Post Process Materials section, click on the "+" button in the Array.
  1. A "Choose" drop-down panel will appear, click on it and select Asset reference.
  1. Click on the drop-down panel labeled "None" and search for "PP_Toon_Inst" or "PP_HandDraw_Inst".

Now, enjoy your beautiful world.

Variables:

Multiple variables are shared between materials, so I'll write it like this:

  • DepthOutlines [bool] (both)

Here you can find the variable name in the editor: "DepthOutlines", the variable type: "[bool]", and for which asset it is: "(both)"

Distance Effect [float] (both)

The distance max of the effect of the post process volume

Light Blend Amount [float] (both)

Intensity of light

Light Tint [Vector3] (both)

The color of the light zone

Shadow Blend Amount [float] (both)

Shadow intensity

Shadow Tint [Vector3] (both)

The color of the shadow zone

Threshold [float] (both)

The threshold between light and shadow zone

Outlines [bool] (both)

Activate Outlines

Depth Outlines [bool] (both)

Activate the depth outline

Normal Outlines [bool] (both)

Activate the Normal outline

Outlines Color [Vector3] (both)

The color of the outline

Ambiant Occlusion [bool] (both)

Use Ambiant Occlusion

Custom Depth [bool] (both)

Enable Post Process only on 3D model on Custom Depth Channel

Shadow Hand Draw Scale [float] (Hand Draw)

The scale of the UV of the Shadow Hand Draw Texture

Shadow Hand Draw Texture [Texture] (Hand Draw)

The texture of shadow
